# Product

## Register

product

## Users

CMOs, executives, and senior operators evaluating AI workflow design for knowledge work. They are judging where AI interfaces reduce coordination cost and where they add another layer of reading, prompting, and supervision.

## Product Purpose

This folder contains explainer artifacts that compare direct GUI, headless agents, and generative UI across workplace journeys. Success means an executive can quickly see which interface pattern fits a given task shape and why the answer is not "make everything a chatbot."

## Brand Personality

Clear, strategic, and skeptical. The voice should sound like a practical advisor speaking to a leadership team, not like a SaaS launch page or AI demo script.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, chatbot-first demos, AI-replaces-every-UI framing, decorative AI futurism, vague productivity claims, and interfaces that make executives read more without helping them decide faster.

## Design Principles

- Match the interface to the work, not to the AI trend.
- Explain the tradeoff in plain English before showing the mockup.
- Treat cognitive load as a cost, especially for executive users.
- Treat tokens, latency, and context reconstruction as product costs, not implementation details.
- Make the winning pattern obvious, but show why the alternatives fail.
- Keep examples grounded in real knowledge-work rituals: docs, standups, roadmaps, marketing plans, analysis loops, and decision reviews.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Design for readable contrast, responsive layouts, keyboard-visible focus, reduced-motion preferences, and plain-language explanations that do not assume deep AI terminology.
